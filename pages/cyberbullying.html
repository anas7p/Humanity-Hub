04 — Cyberbullying

Words on screens leave real wounds

1 in 3 young people has experienced cyberbullying. Behind every screen is a real person.

"Be the reason someone feels welcome, seen and valued."

What cyberbullying is

Cyberbullying includes harassment, exclusion, doxxing, impersonation and spreading rumors online. Its effects on mental health are lasting and serious.

Social media dangers & emotional impact

Constant exposure, anonymity and viral cruelty can lead to anxiety, depression and isolation for those targeted.

Warning signs: withdrawing from friends, avoiding devices, sudden changes in mood, or reluctance to go to school can all signal that someone is being bullied online.

What to actually do about it
  • Don't reply, screenshot and report.
  • Block and mute aggressors.
  • Talk to a trusted adult or counselor.
  • Support the person being targeted.
