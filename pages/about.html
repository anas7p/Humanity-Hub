About

Why this site exists

Humanity Hub started as a small coursework project which then turned into something I actually care about.

The idea

I chose to create a website about these five issues because I wanted to raise awareness that not everyone has the same opportunities or an easy life like many of us do. My goal is to encourage people to remember that, despite our different circumstances and struggles, we are all human and deserve empathy, respect, and support.]

How it came together

Humanity Hub began as a project for my studies at the University of Technology Mauritius (UTM), part of my Secure Front End Application coursework.

What we're trying to do

The goal isn't to lecture anyone. It's to lay out five issues, namely poverty, war, climate change, cyberbullying and mental health as clearly as possible, and point people toward real ways to help.

A note on the sources

Facts and figures across the site are pulled from course material and public sources like the UN, UNHCR and WHO. Numbers change over time, so always double-check current stats before citing them elsewhere.

What matters to us

Three things this site is built on

Awareness

You can't act on a problem you don't understand. Every page starts with the facts.

Empathy

Behind every statistic is a real person. We try not to lose sight of that.

Action

Awareness without action doesn't change much. Every page links to something you can actually do.
