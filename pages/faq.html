About the site

The project

What is Humanity Hub?

It's a small awareness website covering five global issues. Poverty, war & conflict, climate change, cyberbullying and mental health — built as a coursework project at UTM Mauritius.

Are the statistics on this site accurate?

They're based on course material and public sources like the UN, UNHCR and WHO at the time the site was built. Figures like this change often, so treat them as a general picture rather than exact live numbers.

Can I use this site for my own school project?

Feel free to take inspiration from the structure, but write your own content as copying it directly defeats the point of a school project.

About the issues

Poverty, war & climate

What's the difference between poverty and extreme poverty?

Extreme poverty usually refers to living below a set income threshold (the World Bank's line is a common benchmark) and lacking access to basic needs like food, clean water and shelter. Poverty more broadly can include relative poverty within a wealthier country.

Why do refugee crises happen mainly because of war?

Conflict destroys homes, jobs, schools and hospitals, forcing families to leave in search of safety. Climate disasters and persecution also displace people, but armed conflict is currently the single biggest driver.

Can one person actually make a difference on climate change?

Individual choices (diet, transport, energy use) add up, and collective pressure on companies and governments matters even more. The footprint calculator on the climate page is a starting point, not the whole picture.

Cyberbullying & mental health

What should I do if I witness cyberbullying?

Don't join in or share it further. Screenshot it as evidence, report it on the platform, and check in with the person being targeted as knowing someone noticed matters.

Is it normal to feel anxious or low sometimes?

Yes. Everyone experiences stress and low moods. It's worth talking to someone if it starts affecting your daily life, sleep or relationships for an extended period. See the Resources page for hotlines and support links.

Where can I actually get help?

Check the Contact / Resources page for trusted organizations and an emergency contact number.
